Advanced Tactics

💡 Role-Specific Tips

Toggle your role above or use the pill below. Each tip cross-references map and pose strategy.

Painter · Rule #1

Silhouette > Color

Rookies match colors. Veterans break outlines. Choose a non-humanoid pose so your shape reads as "object," not "body."

🔗 See: Pose Tier List
Painter · Rule #2

Hide in the Busy

Hunter instinct #1: check dark corners. Go where they won't look — well-lit, visually noisy backgrounds. Clutter creates camouflage.

🔗 See: Map Explorer for dense zones
Painter · Rule #3

The 3-Color Rule

Every spot needs 3 sampled colors: mid-tone + shadow + highlight. Press V to kill shadows and verify. Single flat color = instant death.

🔗 See: Keybinds — V, Space, 3
Painter · Rule #4

Roughness Is the Silent Killer

#1 forgotten setting. Glossy player on matte wall = visible from across the map. Match roughness and metallic before matching color.

Painter · Rule #5

Clone Mind Games

Place clones to create false silhouettes. In Reverse Chicken Race: your final paint job should be tiny — it's the only one hunters see.

🔗 See: Reverse Chicken Race
Painter · Rule #6

Never Repeat a Spot

Two consecutive rounds in the same spot = you're dead. Good hunters have photographic memory. Rotate maps, rotate angles, stay unpredictable.

Hunter · Rule #1

Hunt Shapes, Not Colors

Elite painters nail colors. You hunt geometry: broken symmetry, odd protrusions, clipping bodies, texture seam mismatches. Color lies — shape doesn't.

Hunter · Rule #2

The Room-Clear System

Sweep clockwise. Start at the perimeter, push inward. Never sprint — visual information blurs at speed. Methodical > fast.

🔗 See: Map Explorer — scan zones marked 🔴 first
Hunter · Rule #3

One Angle Lies

A painter invisible from straight-on might pop out from the side. Crouch, jump, strafe against walls. Change perspective every time you enter a room.

Hunter · Rule #4

Shotgun = Flashlight

The muzzle flash illuminates dark corners better than gamma settings. Every bullet is information — conserve ammo, maximize intel per shot.

Hunter · Rule #5

Slow Down to Speed Up

Sprinting blurs everything. Walk. Stop. Let your eyes adjust. Human vision detects detail best when the observer is stationary. Stand still — let them panic.

Hunter · Rule #6

Ceilings & Floor Edges

Rookie hunters scan eye-level. Pros check ceilings first, floor recesses second. That's where the best painters hide. Look where others don't.

🔗 See: Map Explorer — high & low zones

📋 One-Page Cheat Sheet

Everything you need, one glance. Bookmark this section. Print it. Share it.

🎨 Painter Checklist

  • Pick pose per map (see Tier List)
  • Sample 3 colors minimum
  • Toggle shadows OFF (V) to check
  • Match roughness & metallic
  • X-ray check before time runs out
  • Relocate after whistle (Normal mode)
  • Never same spot twice in a row

🔫 Hunter Checklist

  • Room-clear clockwise, perimeter → in
  • Check ceilings AND floor edges first
  • Change angle: crouch, jump, strafe
  • Look for shape breaks, not colors
  • Use muzzle flash on dark corners
  • Walk — don't sprint while scanning
  • Scan 🔴 zones on Map Explorer first
