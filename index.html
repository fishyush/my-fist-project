Advanced Tips

Toggle role above to see role-specific tips

Tip 01 · Golden Rule

Shape beats color

Noobs obsess over color. Pros fix the silhouette. Use non-humanoid poses to break your outline.

Tip 02

Hide in plain sight

Dark corners are hunter instinct #1. Pick busy, well-lit backgrounds — visual fatigue is your best camouflage.

Tip 03

Sample 3 colors minimum

Mid-tone + shadow + highlight. Toggle shadows OFF (V) before checking. One flat color = instant giveaway.

Tip 04

Mind the roughness

Roughness & metallic sliders are the #1 forgotten setting. A glossy player on a matte wall screams "shoot me."

Tip 05

Clone tactics

Use clones to create geometric illusions. In Reverse Chicken Race, keep your final paint job as small as possible.

Tip 06

Never reuse a spot

Same spot twice = dead. Good hunters remember. Rotate maps, rotate hiding spots, keep them guessing.

Tip 01 · Core Mindset

Forget color. Hunt shapes.

Elite painters match color perfectly. Look for: broken symmetry, odd bumps, clipping bodies, texture seams.

Tip 02

Room-clear method

Sweep clockwise or counter-clockwise. Start at the perimeter, push inward. Never sprint randomly — it blurs vision.

Tip 03

Change angles constantly

One angle lies. Crouch, jump, strafe against walls. A hidden painter pops into view when your perspective shifts.

Tip 04

Shotgun = flashlight

Muzzle flash lights up dark corners better than gamma tweaking. Make every shot count — each bullet gives intel.

Tip 05

Slow is fast

Sprinting blurs everything. Walk. Stop. Let your eyes scan. Human vision detects detail best when stationary.

Tip 06

Look up. Look down.

Rookie hunters never check ceilings or floor edges. Elite painters love high ledges and floor recesses — that's your kill zone.
