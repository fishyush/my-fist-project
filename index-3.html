One-Page Cheat Sheet

The 6 golden rules per role. Screenshot and keep on your phone while you play.

🎨 Painter — 6 Golden Rules
  • Shape > Color. Break your human silhouette first. Match colors second.
  • Hide in plain sight. Dark corners are hunter instinct #1. Clutter is safer.
  • Sample 3 colors. Mid-tone + shadow + highlight. Use the eyedropper (Space).
  • Toggle shadows OFF. Press V before checking. Shadows are instant giveaways.
  • Match roughness. Glossy paint on a matte wall = dead. Adjust the slider.
  • Never reuse a spot. Hunters remember. Rotate hiding spots every round.
🔫 Hunter — 6 Golden Rules
  • Forget color. Hunt shapes. Look for symmetry breaks, odd bumps, clipping.
  • Room-clear method. Sweep clockwise. Perimeter → center. Never sprint randomly.
  • Change angles. Crouch, jump, strafe. One perspective lies.
  • Shotgun = flashlight. Muzzle flash reveals dark corners. Make every shot count.
  • Slow is fast. Walking lets your eyes scan. Sprinting blurs everything.
  • Check ceilings & floors. Rookies never look up or down. That's where pros hide.

Role-Specific

Advanced Tips

Use the 🎨 Painter / 🔫 Hunter toggle at the top to filter. Each role gets 6 curated pro tips.

Tip 01 · Golden Rule

Shape beats color every time

Novices obsess over the perfect hex code. Veterans know: if your outline reads as "human," no color match saves you. Use poses to become geometry.

Tip 02 · Positioning

Hide in plain sight

Dark corners are the first place every hunter checks. Instead, pick well-lit areas with visual complexity — shelves, crates, patterned walls. Let clutter do the work.

Tip 03 · Technique

Three-color minimum

Never paint with one flat color. Sample the mid-tone, the shadow, and the highlight separately. Press V to toggle shadows OFF before your final check.

Tip 04 · Detail

Roughness & metallic matter

These sliders are the #1 forgotten setting. A shiny player on a matte surface is visible from across the map. Match the material, not just the color.

Tip 05 · Clones

Clone psychology

Place clones to create false readings. In Reverse Chicken Race, your most recent paint is revealed — keep it tiny. Let old clones confuse the hunters.

Tip 06 · Longevity

Rotate or die

Never use the same hiding spot twice in a session. Good hunters build a mental map of your habits. Switch maps, switch spots, stay unpredictable.

Tip 01 · Core Mindset

Stop looking for colors

Elite painters match hex codes pixel-perfect. You're hunting shapes: broken symmetry, strange protrusions, clipping body parts, texture seams that don't align.

Tip 02 · Method

The room-clear protocol

Pick a direction (clockwise). Start at the perimeter. Sweep inward. Never sprint randomly — visual information blurs. Slow is smooth, smooth is fast.

Tip 03 · Perspective

One angle always lies

A painter invisible from eye level pops into view when you crouch. Jump. Strafe against walls. Shift your camera constantly — geometry reveals everything.

Tip 04 · Tool Use

Your shotgun is a flashlight

Muzzle flash illuminates dark corners better than any gamma setting. Every bullet should give you intel. Don't spray — place shots deliberately.

Tip 05 · Movement

Stand still to see

Human vision detects movement instantly, but resolves detail best when stationary. Walk. Stop. Scan. Move. Stop. Scan. Sprinting is for the last 10 seconds.

Tip 06 · Blind Spots

Ceilings and floor edges

Rookie hunters have horizontal tunnel vision. Elite painters exploit vertical space — high ledges, ceiling corners, floor recesses. That's your real hunting ground.
